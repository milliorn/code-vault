Anne Rice

  • 1941: Born on October 4 in New Orleans, Louisiana.
  • 1958: Graduates from St. Joseph's Academy in New Orleans.
  • 1960: Attends Texas Woman's University for one year before transferring to Northwestern State University in Louisiana.
  • 1961: Rice marries poet Stan Rice.
  • 1964: Graduates from Northwestern State University with a bachelor's degree in political science and English.
  • 1966: Daughter Michele is born.
  • 1969: Daughter Christine is born.
  • 1970: Son Christopher is born.
  • 1973: "Interview with the Vampire," is published.
  • 1976: "The Feast of All Saints," is published.
  • 1985: "The Vampire Lestat" is published, which becomes a bestseller.
  • 1994: Husband Stan dies of brain cancer.
  • 2002: Converts to Christianity and stops writing about vampires.
  • 2016: "Prince Lestat and the Realms of Atlantis" is published.
  • 2018: "Blood Communion: A Tale of Prince Lestat" is published.
  • 2021: Rice announces that she is developing a TV series based on "Interview with the Vampire."
  • 2021: Passes away December 11, 2021, at the age of 80.
